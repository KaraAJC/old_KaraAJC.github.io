    Questions!

     GitItGurl    NerdLife, feedback, DBCPerfectQs
    Comments    December 7, 2014 10:45PM

    Excuse me, Do you mind if I ask you a question?

    Questions are a vital part of communications. We can ask questions with our face, our body gestures, and of course, our words. How we ask questions can manipulate what the response may be. Ever try asking someone the same question using extremely different body language?

    Knowing this, it's important to have a few tactics for asking questions, so that the answers you recieve are consise, and accurate for what you'd really like to know.

    Here are a few tips for asking a good question:

    • Don't use Jargon: Though it's important to express yourself in a way that your audience will understand, using jargon, or vague wording will quickly confuse people, and keep them from giving you a clear answer. Instead, you may just get more questions!
    • Consider your tone: I believe we don't consider this enough. Even when writing, your tone can get you in trouble, or send your audience in the wrong direction. Always keep in mind how your question may come across, and how to be objective so that others can focus on giving you the information you need.
    • Be specific: It is a pet peeve of mine to get asked questions that have no specificity to them. Ok, what EXACTLY are you looking for? It may be useful to have an example, show a screenshot, etc. let the person know you're putting forth effort to get to a solution, and not just throwing them your work.
    • Be concise: On the other side of the coin, don't start off with your whole life story in order to get an answer that really just needed two sentences. Being considerate of someone's time, and giving them only the information they need to help you out, will allow others to be more forthcoming with an answer.
    As I continue my path into the tech industry, I see countless examples of how these tips can move you forward, or keep you behind. So, I ask you all, What are some ways you choose to ask questions to get the best results?
